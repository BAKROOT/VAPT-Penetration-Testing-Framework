# Authorization to Test (Permission Letter) — Template

> This document grants explicit written permission for security testing. It is sometimes called a "get out of jail free" letter. Keep a signed copy accessible during the entire engagement. Replace all `[bracketed]` fields.

---

**Date:** [date]

**To Whom It May Concern,**

This letter authorizes **[Testing Company / Tester Name]** ("the Tester") to perform authorized security testing, including vulnerability assessment and penetration testing, against the systems and assets owned or operated by **[Client Organization]** ("the Organization") as listed in the approved scope document and Rules of Engagement for engagement **[Engagement ID]**.

## Authorized Scope

The Tester is authorized to assess only the assets explicitly listed as in-scope in the accompanying scope document. Any system not listed is out of scope.

## Authorized Period

This authorization is valid from **[start date/time]** to **[end date/time]** ([timezone]) and may be revoked at any time in writing by the Organization.

## Authority Statement

The undersigned represents that they are authorized to grant this permission on behalf of the Organization and that the Organization owns or has the legal right to authorize testing of the in-scope assets.

## Limitations

- Testing is limited to the agreed scope and techniques in the Rules of Engagement.
- The Tester will not intentionally cause damage or exfiltrate real sensitive data.
- The Tester will follow all emergency stop conditions.
